# Personal Portfolio Website / Academic + Professional Portfolio

## 1. Project Overview

This project is a personal portfolio website designed for academic applications, professional job applications, research networking, and design portfolio presentation.

The website should present my academic research, design works, interdisciplinary projects, technical skills, CV/resume, and selected visual outputs in a clear, modern, interactive, and professional way.

The website should not be a simple static resume page. It should function as a dynamic personal portfolio that can be shared with potential PhD supervisors, academic collaborators, HR recruiters, design firms, planning agencies, sustainability-related employers, and other professional audiences.

The website should be suitable for being included in:

- PhD application CV
- Academic cold emails to potential supervisors
- Job applications
- Professional resume
- Design portfolio PDF
- Email signature
- GitHub profile
- LinkedIn profile
- Academic networking platforms

The overall goal is to build a strong online identity that demonstrates my interdisciplinary profile across landscape architecture, urban ecology, spatial data science, environmental research, design practice, and visual communication.

---

## 2. Core Positioning

This website should be positioned as:

> A dynamic personal portfolio website integrating academic research, landscape design, geospatial analysis, environmental data science, and professional project experience.

It should serve two major purposes:

1. Academic purpose: PhD applications, academic networking, and research communication.
2. Professional purpose: job applications, HR screening, design portfolio presentation, and interdisciplinary career development.

The website should communicate that I am not only a landscape architecture student, nor only a data analyst, but an interdisciplinary researcher and designer who can connect:

- Landscape architecture
- Urban ecology
- Nature-based Solutions
- Climate adaptation and mitigation
- Environmental justice
- Spatial data science
- Remote sensing
- GIS
- Environmental economics
- Design visualization
- Research communication

The core narrative of the website should be:

> My work bridges landscape design, urban ecology, spatial data science, and environmental research to understand how urban natural environments generate ecological, social, and economic values.

## 3. Target Audience

The website should be designed for two main groups of visitors.

### 3.1 Academic Audience

This group includes:

- Potential PhD supervisors
- Admission committee members
- Academic collaborators
- Researchers in landscape architecture, urban ecology, climate adaptation, Nature-based Solutions, environmental justice, and urban planning
- Visitors from academic conferences or research networks

They mainly care about:

- My research interests
- My academic background
- My research trajectory
- My publications and manuscripts
- My methodological skills
- My ability to conduct interdisciplinary research
- My future PhD research potential
- My ability to communicate complex research clearly

For this audience, the website should demonstrate academic seriousness, research continuity, methodological training, and long-term research potential.

### 3.2 Professional Audience

This group includes:

- HR recruiters
- Landscape design firms
- Urban planning agencies
- Sustainability and ESG-related employers
- Environmental consulting firms
- Data analysis or geospatial analysis teams
- Design portfolio reviewers

They mainly care about:

- My design works
- My project experience
- My technical skills
- My visual communication ability
- My software skills
- My ability to turn research into practical design or planning outputs
- My professional adaptability

For this audience, the website should demonstrate design thinking, project execution ability, visual quality, technical skills, and professional readiness.

## 4. Website Goals

The website should help visitors quickly understand:

- Who I am
- What my academic and professional background is
- What research topics I focus on
- What design works I have completed
- What interdisciplinary projects I have worked on
- What methods and software I can use
- How my research and design experiences are connected

The website should emphasize that my strengths include:

- Research capacity
- Design thinking
- Geospatial analysis
- Data visualization
- Environmental and ecological understanding
- Interdisciplinary communication
- Academic writing and systematic review experience
- Ability to integrate research and practice

## 5. Overall Visual and Interaction Style

The website should be modern, professional, interactive, and visually polished.

It should not be too conservative, but it should also not look overly flashy or like a commercial advertising page.

The visual style should be:

- Modern
- Clean
- Professional
- Portfolio-oriented
- Research-oriented
- Visually engaging
- Structured
- Elegant
- Suitable for both academic and professional contexts

The website may include:

- Smooth scrolling
- Subtle animations
- Card hover effects
- Interactive project cards
- Filterable publication cards
- Filterable design portfolio cards
- Timeline animation
- Image gallery
- Image carousel
- Expandable project details
- Smooth page transitions
- Visual research theme map
- Optional interactive data visualizations

The website should avoid:

- Excessive animations
- Overly bright colors
- Slow-loading visual effects
- Unnecessary 3D effects
- Decorative interaction that does not support content
- Dense text blocks without hierarchy
- A purely corporate marketing style
- A plain static resume layout

The most important design principle is:

> Interactions should support communication, not distract from the content.
